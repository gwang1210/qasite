Information for International Students

Washington University prides itself not in our efforts to be more diverse, but to be more cultural aware and knowledgeable. We know in terms of diversity, numbers and facts mean less than the atmosphere and environment that we create here. We are so excited that you have decided to join us in the coming semester, and we want to help you get settled down, to meet new people, to learn, and to thrive. We hope that our FAQ below will answer some questions that you might have.

Transfer FAQs

I am not completely fluent in English. Will this be a big problem?
The short answer is no. The longer answer is that though you may find it troubling to communicate, your English will likely improve, or you can seek help from your fellow international peers or the Office of International Students and Scholars. Reach the OISS here.

How are the cultural groups on campus? Is it easy to join one of them?
Cultural groups on campus are generally very active, with a large genereal body. Many people participate, including students from all over.

Am I allowed days off to observe holidays of my culture?
Yes, but you must notify your professor with acceptable notice.
